Adult learning and hypocrisy

Learning is actively hard; it takes a lot of effort to continue learning past when you're forced to learn. It's much simpler to have information, knowledge, delivered to your feet in the form of a tweet, a text, a headline, rather than exploring then nuances of something new.

Every year, thousands of graduates finish their tenure in the cog of the US educational system and for the first time, have no one forcing them to learn any more if they don't want to. After you finish school - what is your impetus to learn, to grow your mind, to change your way of thinking? If anything, every day you stop actively pushing yourself to learn more, do more, or grow more, the harder it is to get back into the rhythm of learning.

Education (not the manufactured kind you gain in school) in the form of learning is an act so full of momentum. When you start doing it, it becomes easier to learn more - to expand the elastic mental model of your mind. When you stop learning, it becomes so much harder to pick up the skill of learning new skills again.

Funnily enough - you often hear parents making remarks to their kids that are completely misaligned with anything and everything they believe, or do, themselves. When kids are watching slime videos on youtube, you'll hear parents often remark - "go learn something new // go build something // go read something // go learn how to code, etc," oftentimes holding a cell phone in their hands with some for of their own distracted vices pulled up.

Hypocrisy runs deep in society when it comes to learning. Actually, tangentially, hypocrisy runs deep in society when it comes to most good habits. We often try to distance ourselves from the worst of our habits and inability/unwillingness to learn more by pressing our subtle desires to those around us who we care about. I don't think it's a function of anything negative; in fact, I think we do it as a means of escape from ourselves and hope that those we care about don't end up like us: telling our kids to eat healthy while munching on chips; making them read books to learn more while scrolling through instagram ourselves; telling them to work harder in school while trying to figure out how we can leave work before 5 pm; and forcing them to finish multiple degrees in four years while many parents took much longer to finish even one.

Awareness is the first step towards dispelling hypocrisy though, so maybe there's hope after all.
